Raymond Amador

I am a computational physicist. I earned my doctorate at ETH Zürich and Empa, studying chiral reactions on chiral surfaces with enhanced-sampling molecular dynamics and machine-learned interatomic potentials. I build open, visual course companions that teach computational science by doing — implementing the physics, animating it, and validating it against something we already know.

Selected work
